Photosynthesis: Nature's Solar Power

Grade 5 Natural Sciences

Duration: 90 minutes
Grade Level: 5th Grade
Subject: Natural Sciences

Learning Objectives

  • Explain the process of photosynthesis and its importance for life on Earth
  • Identify the key components required for photosynthesis
  • Conduct a simple experiment to demonstrate photosynthesis
  • Connect photosynthesis to everyday life and environmental issues

Key Concepts

What is Photosynthesis?

Photosynthesis is the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy (glucose) using carbon dioxide and water.

Essential Components

  • Sunlight (Light Energy)
  • Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)
  • Water (H₂O)
  • Chlorophyll

Lesson Structure

15 mins

Introduction & Engagement

  • Show fresh plants and ask: "How do plants make their own food?"
  • Brainstorm prior knowledge about plants and sunlight
  • Introduce the term "photosynthesis"
25 mins

Direct Instruction

  • Explain photosynthesis process using visual aids
  • Demonstrate the role of chlorophyll
  • Show animation of the process
30 mins

Guided Practice & Experiment

Leaf Starch Test Experiment

  1. Cover part of a leaf with black paper
  2. Leave plant in sunlight for several hours
  3. Test for starch using iodine
  4. Observe color changes
15 mins

Independent Practice

  • Complete photosynthesis diagram
  • Write explanation in science journals
  • Create concept maps
5 mins

Closure

  • Review key concepts
  • Exit ticket completion
