How to Check Your NSFAS Application Status

A Step-by-Step Guide for Applicants

After submitting your National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) application, it's natural to be eager about the outcome. Checking your application status regularly is important to stay informed about your funding prospects and to ensure you don't miss any important deadlines or requests for additional information. This guide will walk you through the process of checking your NSFAS application status efficiently.

Important: NSFAS application processes and platforms may be updated from time to time. Always refer to the official NSFAS website for the most current information and procedures.

Methods to Check Your NSFAS Application Status

There are several ways to check your NSFAS application status. We'll cover the primary methods below:

1. Checking via the NSFAS Website

Step 1: Visit the NSFAS Website

Open your web browser and go to the official NSFAS website: www.nsfas.org.za

Step 2: Log In to Your Account

  • Click on the "myNSFAS" button at the top right corner of the homepage
  • Enter your username and password
  • Click "Login"

Step 3: Navigate to Application Status

Once logged in, look for a section labeled "Track Funding Progress" or "Application Status"

Step 4: View Your Status

Your current application status should be displayed here. It might be one of the following:

  • Application Submitted
  • Application in Progress
  • Additional Information Required
  • Approved
  • Rejected

2. Checking via SMS

Step 1: Prepare Your Message

Compose an SMS with your ID number

Step 2: Send the SMS

Send the SMS to 32678

Step 3: Wait for Response

You should receive an SMS back with your application status

3. Checking via the NSFAS Contact Centre

Step 1: Prepare Your Information

Have your ID number and application reference number ready

Step 2: Call NSFAS

Dial 08000 67327 (toll-free) during business hours

Step 3: Speak with a Representative

Provide your details and ask about your application status

Tip: Check your application status regularly, especially near key dates like the start of the academic year or during the application review period.

Understanding Your Application Status

Here's what different status updates typically mean:

  • Application Submitted: Your application has been received but not yet reviewed
  • Application in Progress: Your application is being reviewed
  • Additional Information Required: You need to provide more information or documents
  • Approved: Your application for funding has been successful
  • Rejected: Your application was not successful (you may be eligible to appeal)

What to Do If Your Application is "Pending" or "In Progress"

If your application status remains "Pending" or "In Progress" for an extended period:

  • Be patient, as the review process can take time, especially during peak periods
  • Continue checking your status regularly
  • Ensure that NSFAS has your correct contact information
  • Check your email (including spam folders) regularly for any communications from NSFAS

If Additional Information is Required

If your status indicates that additional information is needed:

  • Log in to your myNSFAS account to see what specific information or documents are required
  • Submit the requested information as soon as possible to avoid delays
  • Ensure all documents are clear, legible, and in the correct format
Important: Always respond promptly to requests for additional information. Delays in providing requested documents could affect your funding chances.

After Receiving Your Status

Once you've checked your status:

  • If approved, follow any further instructions provided by NSFAS or your institution
  • If rejected, consider appealing the decision if you believe there's been an error
  • If still pending, continue to monitor your status and respond to any requests promptly

Regularly checking your NSFAS application status keeps you informed and allows you to act quickly if any issues arise or additional information is needed. Stay proactive in monitoring your application, and don't hesitate to contact NSFAS or your institution's financial aid office if you have any questions or concerns about your application status.
