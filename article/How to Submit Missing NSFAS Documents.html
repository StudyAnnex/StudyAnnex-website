How to Submit Missing NSFAS Documents

A Complete Guide for Applicants

If you've applied for NSFAS funding and have been notified that some of your documents are missing, don't panic! This guide will walk you through the process of submitting the required documents to complete your application. Timely submission of missing documents is crucial to ensure your application is processed successfully.

Important: Always check the official NSFAS website or your myNSFAS account for the most up-to-date information on document submission requirements and deadlines.

Understanding Missing Documents

NSFAS may request additional documents for various reasons:

  • To verify your personal information
  • To confirm your financial status or that of your parents/guardians
  • To validate your academic records
  • To clarify any discrepancies in your application

How to Submit Missing Documents

Follow these steps to submit your missing documents to NSFAS:

Document Submission Process

Step 1: Log into your myNSFAS Account

Visit the NSFAS website and log into your myNSFAS account using your username and password.

Step 2: Navigate to the Document Upload Section

Look for a section labeled "Upload Documents" or "Submit Missing Documents" in your account dashboard.

Step 3: Identify Required Documents

Review the list of missing documents requested by NSFAS. Make sure you have digital copies of all required documents ready.

Step 4: Prepare Your Documents

Ensure all documents are clear, legible, and in an accepted format (usually PDF or JPEG). Scan or take clear photos of physical documents if necessary.

Step 5: Upload Documents

Select the appropriate document type from the dropdown menu and upload the corresponding file from your device.

Step 6: Confirm Upload

After each upload, confirm that the document has been successfully submitted. You should see a confirmation message or status update.

Step 7: Review and Submit

Once all documents are uploaded, review your submission and click the final "Submit" or "Confirm" button if required.

Tip: Keep the original documents safe. NSFAS may request to see the originals at a later stage for verification purposes.

Common Missing Documents

Here are some documents frequently requested by NSFAS:

  • Proof of income (e.g., payslips, pension slips)
  • ID documents (yours and your parents'/guardians')
  • Proof of residence
  • Academic records or matric results
  • SASSA grant confirmation (if applicable)
  • Death certificates (in case of deceased parents)

Tips for Successful Document Submission

  1. Submit documents as soon as possible after receiving the request
  2. Ensure all documents are clear, legible, and up-to-date
  3. Double-check that you're uploading the correct document for each requirement
  4. Keep file sizes within the specified limits (usually under 5MB per file)
  5. Use descriptive file names for your documents (e.g., "John_Doe_ID_Document.pdf")
  6. Check your email and myNSFAS account regularly for any follow-up requests

What If You Can't Upload Documents Online?

If you're having trouble uploading documents online, you have alternative options:

  • Contact the NSFAS Call Centre at 0800 067 327 for assistance
  • Visit your institution's Financial Aid Office for help with document submission
  • Check if NSFAS has any walk-in centers in your area for in-person assistance
Remember: Always keep copies of all submitted documents and any correspondence with NSFAS for your records.

Submitting missing documents promptly and correctly is crucial for the successful processing of your NSFAS application. By following these steps and tips, you can ensure that your application is complete and has the best chance of being approved. If you encounter any issues or have questions during the process, don't hesitate to reach out to NSFAS or your institution's financial aid office for assistance.
