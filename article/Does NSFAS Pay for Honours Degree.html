Can NSFAS Pay for Honours Degree?

Everything You Need to Know About NSFAS Funding for Postgraduate Studies

For many South African students, the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) is a crucial lifeline for funding their undergraduate studies. But what happens when you want to continue your education with an Honours degree? This article will explore whether NSFAS can fund your postgraduate studies, specifically an Honours degree, and what you need to know about the process.

Important: NSFAS policies and funding criteria may change from year to year. Always verify the most current information on the official NSFAS website or contact them directly for the most up-to-date details.

The Short Answer: Yes, But With Conditions

Yes, NSFAS can pay for Honours degrees, but there are specific conditions and criteria that must be met. It's important to note that postgraduate funding through NSFAS is not as widely available as undergraduate funding and is subject to stricter requirements.

Eligibility Criteria for NSFAS Honours Funding

Key Requirements for NSFAS Honours Funding

1. South African Citizenship

You must be a South African citizen to be eligible for NSFAS funding at any level.

2. Financial Need

Your combined household income must not exceed R350,000 per annum. This threshold may be subject to change, so always check the current criteria.

3. Academic Performance

You must have completed your undergraduate degree within the minimum time plus one year, and maintained satisfactory academic performance.

4. Continuation in a Scarce Skills Field

Priority is often given to students pursuing Honours degrees in identified scarce skills areas. These may include fields such as science, engineering, technology, and mathematics.

5. Age Limit

There may be age restrictions for postgraduate funding. Check the current NSFAS guidelines for specific age limits.

How to Apply for NSFAS Honours Funding

  1. Check your eligibility based on the criteria mentioned above.
  2. Visit the official NSFAS website and create an account if you don't already have one.
  3. Complete the online application form, providing all required information accurately.
  4. Upload all necessary supporting documents, including proof of acceptance into an Honours program.
  5. Submit your application before the deadline (usually in November for the following academic year).
  6. Track your application status through your NSFAS account.
Tip: Start your application process early. Gathering all required documents and information can take time, and early applicants may have a better chance of securing funding.

What Does NSFAS Honours Funding Cover?

If your application for Honours funding is successful, NSFAS typically covers:

  • Tuition fees
  • Accommodation (if you're not living at home)
  • A limited amount for books and learning materials
  • In some cases, a small personal allowance

Alternative Funding Options for Honours Degrees

If you don't meet the NSFAS criteria for Honours funding or your application is unsuccessful, consider these alternatives:

  • University-specific postgraduate scholarships and bursaries
  • Government department bursaries in your field of study
  • Private sector bursaries and scholarships
  • Research grants (especially for research-intensive Honours programs)
  • Part-time work or work-study programs to support your studies
Remember: Many universities have dedicated postgraduate funding offices. Consult with them for guidance on available funding opportunities specific to your institution and field of study.

While NSFAS does offer funding for Honours degrees, it's important to understand that the process is competitive and funding is limited. Start your research and application process early, maintain excellent academic performance, and explore multiple funding options to increase your chances of securing financial support for your postgraduate studies.
