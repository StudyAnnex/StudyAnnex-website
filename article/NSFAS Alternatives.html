NSFAS Alternatives

Funding Options for South African Students

While the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS) is a vital resource for many South African students, it's not the only option available. Whether you don't qualify for NSFAS, need additional funding, or are exploring all your options, this guide will introduce you to alternative funding sources to support your higher education journey.

Remember: Always verify the legitimacy of any funding opportunity and read all terms and conditions carefully before applying or accepting any financial aid.

Government Bursaries and Scholarships

Various government departments offer bursaries and scholarships for specific fields of study:

  • Department of Basic Education: Funza Lushaka Bursary Programme for teaching students
  • Department of Health: Health and Welfare Sector Education and Training Authority (HWSETA) bursaries
  • Department of Science and Technology: Various STEM-focused bursaries
  • Provincial government bursaries: Check your province's official website for opportunities
Tip: Start your search early and pay attention to application deadlines, which can be as early as a year before your studies begin.

University-Specific Funding

Many universities offer their own bursaries, scholarships, and financial aid packages:

  • Merit-based scholarships for academic, sports, or leadership excellence
  • Need-based bursaries for students from disadvantaged backgrounds
  • Work-study programs that allow you to earn money while studying
  • Subject-specific bursaries for certain courses or faculties

Private Sector Bursaries and Scholarships

Exploring Corporate Funding Opportunities

Step 1: Research Companies in Your Field

Many large corporations offer bursaries or scholarships, often in fields related to their industry. Some examples include:

  • Sasol: Engineering and science bursaries
  • Allan Gray: Orbis Foundation Scholarship for future entrepreneurs
  • Standard Bank: Various bursaries across different fields
  • Vodacom: Technology and IT-related bursaries

Step 2: Check Eligibility Criteria

Each bursary or scholarship will have specific requirements. Common criteria include:

  • Academic performance (often a minimum average is required)
  • Field of study
  • Financial need
  • South African citizenship or permanent residency

Step 3: Prepare a Strong Application

To increase your chances of success:

  • Tailor your application to each opportunity
  • Highlight your achievements and aspirations
  • Provide all required documentation
  • Meet all deadlines

Student Loans

While not ideal, student loans can be a viable option for some students:

  • Banks: Major South African banks offer student loans with varying terms
  • Fundi: Specializes in educational loans and related services
  • Eduloan: Offers loans specifically for education-related expenses
Caution: Carefully consider the long-term implications of taking on debt. Understand the repayment terms and interest rates before committing to a loan.

Crowdfunding and Online Platforms

In the digital age, new funding avenues have emerged:

  • Feenix: A crowdfunding platform specifically for South African students
  • BackaBuddy: Allows you to create campaigns for various causes, including education
  • International platforms: Sites like GoFundMe can be used, but consider currency conversion fees

Part-Time Work and Internships

While not direct funding, earning while you learn can help offset costs:

  • On-campus jobs: Many universities offer part-time work opportunities
  • Internships: Some companies offer paid internships that can help cover expenses
  • Freelancing: Use skills like writing, design, or programming to earn extra income
Tip: Balance work with your studies carefully to ensure your academic performance doesn't suffer.

Tips for Securing Alternative Funding

  • Start your search early, ideally a year before you plan to start studying
  • Apply for multiple opportunities to increase your chances
  • Keep your academic performance strong, as many opportunities are merit-based
  • Maintain a portfolio of your achievements, volunteer work, and extracurricular activities
  • Network and seek mentorship in your field of interest
  • Stay organized and keep track of application deadlines

Remember, funding your education may require a combination of different sources. Be persistent, creative, and proactive in your search for financial support. With determination and the right resources, you can find ways to fund your educational journey and achieve your academic goals.
